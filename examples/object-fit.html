  • Containers: 200 x 200 | 1:1
  • Images orginal size: 700 x 300 | 7:3
  • All images are set in CSS to use
    width: 100%; height: 100%;
    in this example.
CSS:
No object-fit declaration

Trying to force the whole image to fit distorts the image.

CSS:
object-fit: cover

This is like making sure the photo completely covers the frame. It'll zoom in and may cut off parts of the photo to ensure no empty space is showing. The aspect ratio is still maintained. This cropping can sometimes lead to awkward compositions or cut-off important details.

CSS:
object-fit: contain

Think of this like making sure the entire photo fits inside the frame without being cut off. It maintains the photo's aspect ratio, but you might get some empty space around it (like letterboxing on a widescreen movie).
